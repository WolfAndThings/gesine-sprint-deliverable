Foundation Sprint · May 2026
◆ The D2C launch surface and the $5K test that picks the winner
One landing page.
Five paid angles.
Fourteen days to a winner.
The D2C launch lives or dies on two surfaces: the landing page and the five paid angles that drive traffic to it. This document defines both. The page is wireframed section by section with copy starters. The ad test runs $1K per angle for 14 days — a $5K experiment that picks the winning hero, headline, and audience for full launch spend. Win criteria are pre-defined; the data picks the call.

◆ Budget & allocation

$5K, fourteen days, five angles, equal weight.

Equal-weight test, no early reallocation in week 1. Statistical floor: minimum 1,200 link clicks per angle to call a winner with confidence. If an angle is starved by Meta's auction, drop the audience floor before adding budget.

$5,000
◆ Total budget
$1K per angle · 60% Meta · 40% TikTok · 0% Google (held until winning hook is selected for SEM).
14 days
◆ Test duration
Days 1–3: learning · Days 4–10: data collection · Days 11–14: winner selection & scale prep.
1,200
◆ Min clicks / angle
Minimum link clicks required per angle before any kill / scale call is made. Below this, the data is noise.
◆ Win & kill criteria

Pre-decide the rules. Then trust them.

Day-15 decisions are made against this list, not against gut. The angle that meets all four "scale" criteria gets reweighted to 60% of full launch spend; angles meeting all four "kill" criteria are paused.

◆ Scale criteria
When to 4x the budget
CTR > 1.6% on static / VTR @ 3s > 35% on video
ROAS > 1.4 over the last 7 days
CAC < $50 for D2C / < $100 for gifting MQL
Statistical confidence > 90% on lift vs. control
◆ Kill criteria
When to pull the plug
CTR < 0.7% after 1,200 clicks
ROAS < 0.5 over the last 7 days
CAC > $90 for D2C with no improvement trend
Frequency > 4.0 with declining CTR (creative fatigue, not audience size)
◆ 14-day timeline
◆ Week-by-week
Days 1–3Launch all 5 angles. Equal $70/day each. Meta + TikTok learning phase. No edits.
Days 4–7Daily check on CTR + VTR. Refresh creatives only on critical underperformers (frequency > 4 with no engagement).
Days 8–10Mid-test review. Confirm minimum click thresholds met. Begin landing-page heatmap analysis.
Days 11–13Decision window. Apply scale + kill criteria. Identify winner + runner-up.
Day 14Lock winner. Reallocate to 60% of next-month spend. Brief retail buyers + press with the winning angle.
Day 15+Full launch with winner-as-hero, runner-up as retargeting, three losers retired. Repeat the test quarterly.

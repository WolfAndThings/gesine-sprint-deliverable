Why two lanes is not dilution

The risk Ben flagged. The answer that holds.

Single-lane brands die one of two ways: prestige-only brands hit a margin ceiling and never get to volume, mainstream-only brands hit a price ceiling and never get acquired. The two-lane structure was already proven by the cleanest exits in adjacent categories.

The pattern

Every brand that cleared $500M+ in adjacent categories ran both lanes.

The prestige lane defends the brand's design and copy moat. The mainstream lane unlocks distribution scale. Acquirers pay for the prestige multiple but they buy the volume. Running just Path A caps GESINE at the Aesop ceiling without the volume to justify it. Running just Path B sells through Target but never gets the Aesop multiple. The two lanes are the architecture, not a hedge.

Sequencing · what runs when

Path A first. Path B follows.

The Foundation Sprint locks Path A. Path B is built on top of the design-and-fragrance system Path A creates. Wrong order kills the brand. Launching mainstream first installs the brand at the wrong price anchor and forecloses Sephora.

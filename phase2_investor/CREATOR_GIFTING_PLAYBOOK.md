## Send package contents

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Hero bottle (1) | The product itself, in flagship colorway (Smoked Sage) |
| Vial sample set (3 scents) | Lets them experiment, find favorite |
| Branded ritual card | One-page ritual instructions, not marketing |
| Personal handwritten note from Lee | Founder hand. No printed letter. |
| Custom UTM-tagged discount code | For tracking attribution + their followers |
| Wood gift box, tied with linen ribbon | The unboxing IS the content |

Cost per kit: target $35-50 landed. Premium-feeling, sustainable.

## What NOT to include

- Sales sheet
- Press release
- Branded swag (tote, pen, pin) - cheapens it
- "Posting requirements" letter - kills authenticity

## Outreach script (DM or email)

```
{{first_name}},

Built something I think fits how you live with your home.

Mailing a small ritual kit. No ask, no required post. If you use it, that is plenty.

Address?

Lee
GESINE
```

Three sentences. No links. No deck.

## Tracking

Sheet: creator handle, platform, sent date, kit received date, organic mention y/n, mention type (story/post/reel/TikTok), reach (estimated), click attribution via UTM, comments captured.

## Founder voice rule

Every sent kit is from Lee. Not from "the team." Not from a PR agency. The founder's hand is the differentiator. Goop did this. Aesop did this. Drunk Elephant did this. None of them used a publicist for the first 100 sends.

## Sourcing flow

1. Pull creator from sheet (start with Tier 1 named above).
2. Verify last 30 days posted (not dormant).
3. Verify audience overlap (followers' lookalikes match ICP).
4. DM or email the address request - direct, short, no pitch.
5. On receipt confirmation, ship within 48h.
6. No follow-up after delivery. If they post, they post.

## Why this works for investors

Creator-led GTM is a low-CAC channel for Modern Premium DTC. Reference points (all from [research/teardowns_and_exits_v2.md](research/teardowns_and_exits_v2.md) and [research/market_sizing.md](research/market_sizing.md)):

- **Drunk Elephant**: ~$100M revenue by 2019, 7 years post-founding (2012); Sephora launch Jan 2015 was the major retail flywheel — not creator gifting alone. Acquired by Shiseido 2019 for $845M (8.5x revenue). Source: [Shiseido investor PDF](https://corp.shiseido.com/en/ir/pdf/ir20191008_646.pdf), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-10-08/shiseido-inks-845-million-deal-for-skincare-firm-drunk-elephant).
- **Hero Cosmetics**: ~$115M trailing-12-month net sales at acquisition (Aug 2022), founded 2017 — ~5 years to acquisition. Acquired by Church & Dwight for $630M (~5.5x revenue). Source: [BeautyMatter](https://beautymatter.com/articles/church-dwight-acquires-hero-cosmetics-in-630-million-deal), [Business Wire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20220906005426/en/). The "creator-led TikTok" framing is partial — Hero scaled primarily through Amazon + Target. Adjust framing.
- **Bala**: ~$29M annual revenue (2025 update per Shark Tank Insights); some sources cite $50M (2023) and $64M lifetime sales by 2024 — figures conflict and are not audited. Source: [research/teardowns_and_exits_v2.md](research/teardowns_and_exits_v2.md).

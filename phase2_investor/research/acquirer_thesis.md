## 1. Church & Dwight (CHD)

**Thesis (~80 words).** Most disciplined strategic buyer in mid-cap CPG. Public criteria: #1 or #2 brand in category, asset-light, growing, gross-margin accretive. Has executed twice on this exact playbook in five years (Hero in acne patches, Touchland in hand sanitizer). CEO Rick Dierker (April 2025) and CFO Lee McChesney explicitly named M&A as primary cash-use priority on Q3 2025 call. Power-brand strategy means C&D wants founder-led category disrupters they can scale via existing distribution.

**Recent acquisitions:**

| Brand | Year | Price | TTM rev / EBITDA at close | Category | Strategic logic |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Touchland | 2025 | $700M + $180M earn-out (up to $880M) | $130M / $55M EBITDA | Hand sanitizer | #2 in category, premium up-trade, founder retained |
| Hero Cosmetics | 2022 | $630M | $115M / $45M EBITDA | Acne patches | #1 patch brand, asset-light, 40% EBITDA margin |
| Therabreath | 2021 | $580M | Not disclosed | Oral rinse | #1 alcohol-free mouthwash |
| Zicam (Matrixx) | 2020 | $530M | Not disclosed | Cold remedy | Power-brand criteria |
| Waterpik | 2017 | $1.0B | Not disclosed | Oral care device | Premium device platform |
| Drybar consumer products | 2020 | $255M | Not disclosed | Hair styling | Premium prestige play |
| Toppik (Spectrum) | 2018 | Not disclosed | Not disclosed | Hair fibers | Niche category leader |

**What they want next.** McChesney on Q3 2025 call: M&A remains primary cash use. Dierker: "remain active in evaluating the right acquisition opportunities." Active divestiture of VitaFusion / L'il Critters signals concentration on power brands. Pattern points to next bolt-on at $100M-$300M revenue scale.

**GESINE fit.** GESINE is structurally identical to a 2022-vintage Touchland or Hero: founder-led, premium up-trade, capsule-refill platform technology with margin profile that survives scale. Home-reset ritual is an open white space (no #1 incumbent). Caraway-firm design and EWG Verified mirror the Touchland aesthetic-meets-clean playbook C&D paid 16x EBITDA for.

**Likelihood: 5/5.** Most pattern-matched buyer in the universe. The only friction is timing. C&D needs to digest Touchland integration through 2026-2027 before opening another $500M+ ticket. Target window 2028-2030.

**What they want next.** "Bolt-on acquisitions in premium segments." Wild proves refillable-format fluency. Dr. Squatch proves taste for design-led clean personal care. Recent Q1 2025 results call out Beauty & Wellbeing as growth engine.

**GESINE fit.** Sits at the literal intersection of Seventh Generation (eco home) and Wild (refillable platform) and Tatcha (prestige clean ritual). Farmacy, Ouai, Tula in 2021 extended into prestige. Wonderbelly (Jan 2026) signals continued appetite for clean-label modern brands. CEO has publicly said P&G is "open to acquisitions in consumer health care." Methodical, patient, prefers proven retail traction.

**What they want next.** Wonderbelly thesis: "ready-made, clean-label brand built for modern retail shelves and social-media-friendly marketing." CEO public on consumer-health appetite. ## 4. SC Johnson

**Thesis (~80 words).** The category-correct buyer for premium home and cleaning. Built Lifestyle Brands division explicitly to house eco-premium acquisitions: Caldrea + Mrs. Meyer's (2008), Babyganics (2016), Method + Ecover (2018, from People Against Dirty). Family-owned, low M&A frequency, but when SCJ buys, they buy the category-defining premium brand. Lifestyle Brands now headquartered in San Francisco with 650 employees. SCJ is the only acquirer where home-reset ritual IS the core thesis.

**Recent acquisitions:**

| Brand | Year | Price | Category | Strategic logic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Method + Ecover | 2018 | Not disclosed | Premium eco cleaning | Category leader, design-led |
| Babyganics | 2016 | Not disclosed | Baby clean care | Adjacent-demographic premium |
| Caldrea / Mrs. Meyer's | 2008 | Not disclosed | Premium home fragrance / cleaning | Foundation of LSB |

**What they want next.** No public M&A roadmap (private company). But SCJ has not added to Lifestyle Brands portfolio since 2018 — a 7-year gap suggests they are due, and have likely been building the next acquisition through observation. Method has run its premium-cleaning thesis; Mrs. Meyer's has aged. SCJ needs the next-gen design-led home-care brand to refresh the LSB stack.

**GESINE fit.** Closest single-brand fit in the universe. GESINE is essentially Method's evolution: refillable platform replaces refill pouches, ritual replaces utility, capsule format is a true product innovation Method never had. Caraway-firm design language is a peer to Method's original Karim Rashid bottle work. Crate & Barrel 2027 mirrors how Method launched at Target.

**Likelihood: 5/5.** Highest single-brand fit. Risk is SCJ M&A cadence is slow and private — they may take longer to act, and price discipline is unknown (no public comp). Target window 2029-2032.

## Acquirer Target Stack (synthesis)

GESINE has two-axis acquirer math: **brand-thesis fit** (does the buyer want premium home-reset ritual?) and **timing readiness** (is the buyer cash-deployable in 2028-2032?). Five-acquirer ranked target list:

1. **SC Johnson** (5/5 fit). Single-best category match. Method's evolution thesis lands directly. Refresh-cycle math says SCJ is overdue for a Lifestyle Brands addition. Risk: private, opaque, deliberate. **Target window 2029-2032.** First contact via Method/Mrs. Meyer's BD network and SCJ Lifestyle Brands SF office.

2. **Church & Dwight** (5/5 fit). Most disciplined buyer in CPG with public criteria GESINE will satisfy by 2028 (#1 or #2 brand in premium home reset, asset-light, 35%+ EBITDA margin, growing). C&D needs to digest Touchland integration first; M&A pipeline reopens 2028-2029. **Target window 2028-2030.** First contact via C&D corporate development team post-Touchland-integration milestone (2027 earnings).

3. **Unilever** (4/5 fit). Wild + Dr. Squatch + Seventh Generation playbook converges directly on GESINE. Brand fit very high; revenue threshold ($300M+ for new platform plays) higher. **Target window 2030-2032** at $50M-$100M revenue scale. First contact via Unilever Prestige BD (Mary Carmen Gasco-Buisson team) plus Personal Care.

4. **L Catterton** (4/5 as bridge / minority). PE bridge to strategic. Provides growth capital at $25M-$50M revenue and runway to strategic-buyer threshold. Best near-term strategic option. **Target window 2026-2028** for minority PIPE or majority growth round.

5. **L'Oréal** (4/5 fit, premium-priced). The Aesop comp is exact, and L'Oréal pays the highest multiples in beauty.

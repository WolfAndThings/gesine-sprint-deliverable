Research output for GESINE Phase 2 investor deliverable. Every tactical specific cited to a primary or near-primary URL. Items that are widely repeated but lack a clean primary citation are flagged as such.

## 1. Method (2001 → SC Johnson, 2017)

### A. Origin
**Founders:** Adam Lowry (chemical engineer, Stanford) and Eric Ryan (advertising/marketing, University of Rhode Island), San Francisco roommates. **Founded:** Method Products incorporated 2000, first products on shelves 2001. **Founding capital:** Roughly $90,000 in personal savings + friends-and-family money to start; in 2001, raised ~$1M from private investors including former Yahoo chairman Tim Koogle "at a time when they had $16 in the bank and $300,000 on their credit cards." Source: [Encyclopedia.com profile of Lowry & Ryan](https://www.encyclopedia.com/journals/culture-magazines/lowry-adam-and-ryan-eric); [SBN Online](https://sbnonline.com/article/how-eric-ryan-and-adam-lowry-have-built-a-bright-future-with-the-green-cleaning-supplies-of-method-products/). **Brand thesis at founding:** the cleaning aisle treats dirt as the enemy and design as an afterthought; build biodegradable, design-forward home care that people are happy to leave on the counter.

### B. The 5 actual growth tactics

1. **Hire Karim Rashid as Chief Creative Officer in 2001 and ship a sculptural dish-soap bottle that became the brand's identity.** Rashid designed a bottom-dispensing, bowling-pin-shaped clear bottle that won an I.D. Magazine Annual Design Review Distinction. The bottle was the core of Method's national pitch deck. Source: [Campaign — Champions of Design: Method](https://www.campaignlive.co.uk/article/champions-design-method/1074784); [CNN/Money — Method sells cool in a bottle, Jan 2004](https://money.cnn.com/2004/01/28/technology/business2_coolbottle/).

2. **First retail account = Mollie Stone's Market (suburban San Francisco), 2001 — two shelves of all-purpose spray cleaner.** Tiny but essential proof-of-concept that funded the next pitch. Source: [Encyclopedia.com](https://www.encyclopedia.com/journals/culture-magazines/lowry-adam-and-ryan-eric).

3. **Target 90-store trial in 2002 → expansion to 1,000+ Target stores in 2003.** This is the move that took Method from indie-grocery curiosity to national brand; by 2003 Method was in ~10,000 stores doing ~$10M annual sales. Source: [Campaign — Champions of Design: Method](https://www.campaignlive.co.uk/article/champions-design-method/1074784); [SBN Online](https://sbnonline.com/article/how-eric-ryan-and-adam-lowry-have-built-a-bright-future-with-the-green-cleaning-supplies-of-method-products/).

4. **Co-founded the B Corp movement (May 2007 certification).** Method was one of the original B Corp signatories — turned sustainability claims into a third-party-verified competitive moat against legacy CPG. In 2017 Method + Ecover hit a B Impact score of 146 (top 2%). Source: [PR Newswire — Method and Ecover B Impact Assessment 2017](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/method-and-ecover-deliver-outstanding-b-impact-assessment-score-for-2017-300471518.html); [B Corporation directory — People Against Dirty](https://bcorporation.com.au/directory/people-against-dirty).

5. **"People Against Dirty" brand voice + activist marketing.** Method positioned the entire category — not just competing brands — as the enemy ("dirty business"), letting them recruit a values-driven customer base instead of competing on price. The voice carried into packaging copy, ads, and the company's own corporate identity (parent co. is literally named "People Against Dirty"). Source: [Fortune — Mopping Up With Method](https://fortune.com/2013/10/10/mopping-up-with-method/).

### C. Acquisition
**Buyer:** Ecover (2012) → SC Johnson (2017). **Price:** Both undisclosed. **Revenue at sale:** ~$112M in 2012 (Method standalone, Happi Top 50); combined Method + Ecover ~$200M at SC Johnson sale. Source: [Happi — Method sold to Ecover](https://www.happi.com/contents/view_breaking-news/2012-09-06/method-sold-to-ecover/); [SC Johnson press release Sept 2017](https://www.scjohnson.com/en/press-releases/2017/september/sc-johnson-signs-agreement-to-acquire-method-and-ecover). **Why the buyer wanted them:** SC Johnson needed a sustainability-credentialed brand portfolio to defend against shifting consumer preferences in home care. Source: [Sustainable Brands](https://sustainablebrands.com/read/sc-johnson-expands-sustainable-product-portfolio-acquires-method-ecover).

### D. GESINE lesson
Hire one senior design name early and let one signature object (Method's bottle) carry the brand through the door. A category-defining product silhouette is a faster pitch than a tagline.

## 2. Touchland (2018 → Church & Dwight, 2025)

### A. Origin
**Founder:** Andrea Lisbona (Barcelona-born, relocated to Miami). **Founded:** Brand began in Spain ~2010; relaunched in U.S. with Kickstarter in 2018. **Founding capital:** Bootstrapped + Kickstarter; total external funding only $1.5M across 1 round (per Tracxn). Source: [Inc. — How Touchland Scored a $700M Sale](https://www.inc.com/kevin-j-ryan/touchland-hand-sanitizer-700-million-sale-church-dwight/91220361); [Tracxn — Touchland profile](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/touchland). **Brand thesis at founding:** hand sanitizer is a commodity drugstore product — make it a fragrance-led beauty SKU you want to display, not hide.

### B. The 5 actual growth tactics

1. **Kickstarter, 2018: 1,304 backers, $67,622 raised against $15K goal (450% of goal, 150% in first 24 hours).** Manufactured demand and generated the proof Sephora later required. Source: [Touchland Kickstarter project page](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/959783808/touchland-the-next-generation-of-hand-sanitizers); [Create + Cultivate interview with Andrea Lisbona](https://www.createcultivate.com/blog/2024/1/27/touchland-founder-andrea-lisbona-believes-in-the-power-of-kickstarter).

2. **Sephora endcap placement starting 2019, expanding to 2,500+ U.S. retail doors by 2021 (Sephora, Ulta, Urban Outfitters, Bloomingdale's, Anthropologie).** A hand sanitizer in the Sephora beauty assortment was itself a category-bending media event. Source: [Happi — Touchland Power Mist at Sephora endcaps](https://www.happi.com/breaking-news/touchland-rsquo-s-power-mist-moisturizing-hand-san/); [WWD — Touchland to Enter Target and Sephora, Launches Glow Mist](https://wwd.com/beauty-industry-news/beauty-features/touchland-launches-anti-aging-sanitizer-goes-into-target-and-sephora-1235017322/).

3. **TikTok-native product design + #Touchland hashtag past 133.3M views.** The square aluminum-look bottle in pastel colorways was engineered for the FYP — content creators showed it as an accessory, not a hygiene SKU. Source: [Glossy — How Touchland uses social media to make hand sanitizer a sell-out moment](https://www.glossy.co/beauty/touchland-hand-sanitizer-social-media-blackpink/).

4. **IP collaboration drops as scarcity engine: Disney (2021), Smiley (2022), Hello Kitty (2024), BLACKPINK (2024).** The BLACKPINK Blue Sandalwood Power Mist sold out in 5 days at Ulta + touchland.com and built a 5,600+ person waitlist. Source: [Billboard — BLACKPINK & Touchland](https://www.billboard.com/culture/product-recommendations/blackpink-touchland-collaboration-1235352178/); [Glossy](https://www.glossy.co/beauty/touchland-hand-sanitizer-social-media-blackpink/).

5. **Compounding revenue trajectory tied to retail expansion: ~$12M in 2020 → 163% growth Q1 2023 → 203% growth FY2023 → $100M+ in 2024 → $130M TTM at acquisition (Mar 2025).** This wasn't one viral moment — it was a 5-year drumbeat of double- and triple-digit growth that justified the multiple. Source: [Cosmetics Design — Touchland 123% Q2 sales surge](https://www.cosmeticsdesign.com/Article/2024/09/06/Touchland-s-123-Q2-sales-surge-A-blueprint-for-personal-care-brands/); [GCI Magazine — 163% Q1 2023 gain](https://www.gcimagazine.com/brands-products/bath-body/news/22847087/how-touchland-achieved-163-sales-gains-in-q1-2023); [Business Wire — Church & Dwight to acquire Touchland](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250512684279/en/Church-Dwight-to-Acquire-the-Touchland-Brand-for-$700-million-Plus-Earn-out).

### C. Acquisition
**Buyer:** Church & Dwight. **Year:** Announced May 12, 2025; closed Q2 2025. **Price:** $700M cash + restricted stock at close, plus up to $180M earn-out tied to 2025 net sales = up to $880M total. **Revenue at sale:** ~$130M TTM through March 31, 2025; ~$55M EBITDA (~42% margin). **Multiple:** ~5.4x revenue at base; up to ~6.8x including earn-out. Source: [Church & Dwight investor relations release](https://investor.churchdwight.com/Investors/news/news-details/2025/Church--Dwight-to-Acquire-the-Touchland-Brand-for-700-million-Plus-Earn-out/default.aspx); [Business Wire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250512684279/en/Church-Dwight-to-Acquire-the-Touchland-Brand-for-$700-million-Plus-Earn-out). **Why the buyer wanted them:** #2 hand sanitizer brand in the U.S., the fastest-growing brand in the category, double-digit growth runway, founder-led management staying post-deal. Same source.

### D. GESINE lesson
Engineer the SKU itself for the social platform you need — Touchland's bottle is a TikTok prop first, sanitizer second. GESINE's primary object should be designed to be filmed in a creator's hand, not just to function on a counter.

## 3. Hero Cosmetics (2017 → Church & Dwight, 2022)

### A. Origin
**Founders:** Ju Rhyu (CEO), Dwight Lee, Andrew Lee. **Founded:** 2017. **Founding capital:** $50,000 pooled by the three co-founders. Source: [Fortune — How Hero Cosmetics' CEO made pimple patches a Gen Z status symbol](https://fortune.com/2024/06/30/ju-rhyu-hero-cosmetics-gen-z-pimple-patches-secrets-to-success/). **Brand thesis at founding:** import the K-beauty hydrocolloid pimple patch (Rhyu discovered it as an expat in Seoul working at Samsung) to a U.S. market where acne was treated medically, not playfully.

### B. The 5 actual growth tactics

1. **Amazon-first launch with intentional Prime-eligibility pricing.** Rhyu wanted to price Mighty Patch at $9.99 but couldn't unlock Prime; raised it to $12.99, qualified for Prime, and the first batch sold out in 90 days. Prime alone is reported to lift orders ~25%. Source: [Fortune — Mighty Patch sold out in 90 days, Amazon Prime "accident"](https://fortune.com/2024/07/08/k-beauty-mighty-patch-pimple-hydrocolloid-hero-cosmetics-ju-rhyu-amazon-prime/).

2. **Dominate Amazon's "acne patch" search SERP — go all-in on one channel before diversifying.** Hero's Amazon dominance built the trust signal and review volume that later made retailers come to them. Source: [Female Startup Club podcast with Ju Rhyu](https://www.femalestartupclub.com/blogs/podcasts/ju-rhyu-hero-cosmetics).

3. **TikTok #SkinTok virality powered by the "peel-off reveal" mechanic.** Mighty Patch was inherently a content product — creators peel the patch and reveal the absorbed gunk underneath. Rhyu credits social as the key driver of the brand's popularity. Source: [Fortune — Gen Z status symbol](https://fortune.com/2024/06/30/ju-rhyu-hero-cosmetics-gen-z-pimple-patches-secrets-to-success/); [Glossy 50 2022 — Ju Rhyu](https://www.glossy.co/beauty/ju-rhyu-hero-cosmetics-glossy-50/).

4. **Target launch September 2019: 1,521 doors with a SKU built specifically for that retailer ($7 Mighty Patch Duo: 6 Original + 6 Invisible+).** Designed an exclusive retail-channel pack rather than dumping the DTC SKU into mass. Revenue grew 300%+ in 2020 alone after the Target launch. Source: [Beauty Independent — Mighty Patch bursts into Target](https://www.beautyindependent.com/hero-cosmetics-mighty-patch-target/); [PR Newswire — Hero Cosmetics growth investment & distribution](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hero-cosmetics-receives-growth-investment-and-announces-product-expansion-and-new-distribution-partners-301205408.html).

5. **Stay narrow until you own the category, then expand.** Hero stayed purely on hydrocolloid acne patches until they had the #1 acne-patch + #2 acne-brand position in the U.S.; only then did they expand into adjacent SKUs (cleansing bars, dark spot patches). Over 1 billion patches sold to date. Source: [Fortune — Gen Z status symbol](https://fortune.com/2024/06/30/ju-rhyu-hero-cosmetics-gen-z-pimple-patches-secrets-to-success/); [BeautyMatter — Church & Dwight acquires Hero for $630M](https://beautymatter.com/articles/church-dwight-acquires-hero-cosmetics-in-630-million-deal).

### C. Acquisition
**Buyer:** Church & Dwight. **Year:** Announced Aug 31, 2022; closed Oct 13, 2022. **Price:** $630M (~10% in restricted founder stock). **Revenue at sale:** ~$115M TTM net sales. **EBITDA:** ~$45M (~40% margin). **Multiple:** ~5.5x revenue, ~14x EBITDA. Source: [Business Wire — Church & Dwight to acquire Hero Cosmetics](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20220906005426/en/); [BeautyMatter](https://beautymatter.com/articles/church-dwight-acquires-hero-cosmetics-in-630-million-deal). **Why the buyer wanted them:** #1 acne-patch brand, #2 U.S. acne brand overall, Gen Z entry point, 40%+ EBITDA margins, and founders committed to stay.

### D. GESINE lesson
Win one channel completely before adding any others. Hero's "Amazon SERP dominance first, retail second" sequence is a more disciplined playbook than launching everywhere at once and signals operational discipline to acquirers.

## 4. Drunk Elephant (2012 → Shiseido, 2019)

### A. Origin
**Founder:** Tiffany Masterson, Houston-based mother of four, no prior beauty industry background. **Founded:** Concept 2012; brand officially launched August 2013. **Founding capital:** Bootstrapped — no public report of a friends-and-family round. First outside capital was a minority investment from VMG Partners + Leandra Medine in March 2017, reportedly ~$8.33M. Source: [Fashionista — How Drunk Elephant Founder Went From Stay-at-Home Mom to Beauty Mogul](https://fashionista.com/2018/03/tiffany-masterson-drunk-elephant-skincare-sephora); [WWD — Drunk Elephant Gets Investment From VMG Partners, Leandra Medine](https://wwd.com/beauty-industry-news/skin-care/feature/drunk-elephant-leandra-medine-man-repeller-vmg-partners-invesment-10830910/). **Brand thesis at founding:** "clean-compatible" skincare — eliminate six ingredient classes Masterson identified as the root of her own skin issues, replace them with biocompatible actives skin recognizes.

### B. The 5 actual growth tactics

1. **The "Suspicious 6" manifesto: essential oils, drying alcohols, silicones, chemical sunscreens, fragrances/dyes, SLS.** A bannable, repeatable, trademarked list became the brand's spine. It defined enemies, made the product instantly explainable, and let consumers self-identify as "Suspicious-6-free" loyalists. Source: [FOLIO.YVR — Tiffany Masterson's Suspicious 6](https://folioyvr.com/2025/08/tiffany-mastersons-suspicious-6-drunk-elephant/); [Drunk Elephant homepage](https://www.drunkelephant.com/).

2. **2014 Cosmoprof trade show → Sephora discovery → January 2015 Sephora launch.** Masterson didn't pitch Sephora — she went to a trade show, was spotted, and was invited in. Became one of Sephora's top-selling and fastest-growing skincare brands almost immediately. Source: [Fashionista](https://fashionista.com/2018/03/tiffany-masterson-drunk-elephant-skincare-sephora).

3. **"Biocompatible" positioning that rejected the binary "natural vs. synthetic" frame.** Drunk Elephant publicly argued that "natural" doesn't automatically mean good — every ingredient must directly benefit skin health or formulation safety. This intellectual repositioning gave Sephora consumers a third option between Whole Foods naturals and prestige synthetics. Source: [Drunk Elephant homepage / Philosophy](https://www.drunkelephant.com/); [Drunk Elephant Malaysia philosophy page](https://drunkelephant.my/pages/philosophy).

4. **The "smoothie" routine concept and "Trunk" merchandising bundle.** Reframed skincare as a mix-and-match cocktail rather than a fixed routine; the Trunk bundle gave Sephora a high-AOV gifting/holiday SKU. Source: [Space NK — Drunk Elephant Trunk 5.0 product page](https://www.spacenk.com/us/skincare/skincare-gift-sets/skincare-set/trunk-5.0-MUK200032316.html).

5. **Hero product anchor: Virgin Marula Luxury Facial Oil.** A single signature SKU carried the brand identity (Marula → "Drunk Elephant" name itself) and gave press, influencers, and gifters one thing to talk about. Won Influenster's 2018 Reviewers' Choice and 2019 Best Indie Facial Oil. Source: [Sephora — Virgin Marula Luxury Face Oil product page](https://www.sephora.com/product/virgin-marula-tm-luxury-facial-oil-P392245); [Influenster review hub](https://www.influenster.com/reviews/drunk-elephant-virgin-marula-luxury-facial-oil). _Note: I did not find a clean primary source confirming a formal "Sephora Squad" ambassador program for Drunk Elephant — widely referenced but no primary citation found in this pass; flag if needed._

### C. Acquisition
**Buyer:** Shiseido (via Shiseido Americas). **Year:** Announced Oct 8, 2019; closed Nov 6, 2019. **Price:** $845M. **Revenue at sale:** ~$100M (with Shiseido citing 2019 net sales of ~$120M). **Multiple:** ~8.5x revenue — among the highest multiples paid for a premium-DTC beauty brand. Source: [Bloomberg — Shiseido Inks $845M Deal](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-10-08/shiseido-inks-845-million-deal-for-skincare-firm-drunk-elephant); [Shiseido press release](https://corp.shiseido.com/en/news/detail.html?n=00000000002773); [Shiseido investor PDF](https://corp.shiseido.com/en/ir/pdf/ir20191008_646.pdf). **Why the buyer wanted them:** Premium clean-beauty anchor for a Japanese giant with no comparable U.S. prestige brand; cult Gen Z + Millennial loyalty; instant Sephora prestige presence.

### D. GESINE lesson
Name your enemies. The "Suspicious 6" gave Drunk Elephant a manifesto a customer could repeat at a dinner party. GESINE needs an equivalent banned-list — a short, memorable enumeration of what luxury cleaning has been doing wrong (synthetic fragrance, plastic refills, "cleaning theater" marketing) that a customer can recite back.

## 5. Dollar Shave Club (2011 → Unilever, 2016)

### A. Origin
**Founder:** Michael Dubin (CEO, ex-improv comedian, ex-Sports Illustrated digital). Co-founder: Mark Levine (met at a holiday party where Levine mentioned having a warehouse of cheap razors). **Founded:** Concept Jan 2011; service launched March 6, 2012. **Founding capital:** $1M seed in March 2012 from Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers, Andreessen Horowitz, Shasta Ventures, Forerunner Ventures and others; $9.8M Series A in October 2012 (same group + Venrock). Source: [Wikipedia — Dollar Shave Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dollar_Shave_Club); [TechCrunch — Dollar Shave Club Raises $75M (2015 Series D coverage with funding history)](https://techcrunch.com/2015/06/22/dollar-shave-club-raises-75mm-to-fend-off-gillette-and-harrys/). **Brand thesis at founding:** monthly subscription delivers a "good enough" razor cheaper than Gillette's blade-in-a-locked-case retail model.

### B. The 5 actual growth tactics

1. **The $4,500 viral launch video, March 6, 2012.** Written by and starring Dubin, shot in one day. Within 48 hours: 12,000 new subscribers; the YouTube traffic crashed the company's server in the first hour. Won "Best Out-of-Nowhere Video Campaign" at the 2012 Ad Age Viral Video Awards. Now sits past 27M views (as of Nov 2020). Source: [Inc. — How a $4,500 YouTube Video Turned Into a $1B Company](https://www.inc.com/magazine/201707/lindsay-blakely/how-i-did-it-michael-dubin-dollar-shave-club.html); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dollar_Shave_Club).

2. **Subscription model with a $1/month entry tier.** The "$1" headline was the hook; the unit economics worked off the $6 and $9 tiers. The subscription mechanic compounded subscriber count to 3.2M by acquisition. Source: [Retail Dive — Unilever acquires DSC for $1B](https://www.retaildive.com/news/unilever-acquires-subscription-startup-dollar-shave-club-for-1b/423029/); [Fortune — Unilever Buys Dollar Shave Club for $1 Billion](https://fortune.com/2016/07/19/unilever-buys-dollar-shave-club-for-1-billion/).

3. **"Bathroom Minutes" — a printed mini-magazine in every shipment (2014–2015).** ~10-page editorial newsletter combining articles, product news, and community signals. Turned a delivery box into a media moment and meaningfully reduced churn. Source: [Contently — Dollar Shave Club Wants to Keep You Reading on the Toilet](https://contently.com/2015/05/26/dollar-shave-club-wants-to-keep-you-reading-on-the-toilet/).

4. **MEL Magazine launched late 2015 — full editorial site for men's content.** Long-form pieces ("I Went Shark Fishing and Accidentally Caught a Kilo of Coke"), short documentaries, and a real masthead — DSC became a media company that happened to sell razors. Source: [Convince & Convert — How Storytelling Turned DSC Into a Billion-Dollar Brand](https://www.convinceandconvert.com/content-marketing/storytelling-turned-dollar-shave-club-into-a-billion-dollar-brand/).

5. **Refer-a-friend program: $5 credit per successful referral, prompted right after first purchase.** Drove ~50,000 referrals/month at peak. Source: [Latterly — Dollar Shave Club Marketing Strategy Case Study](https://www.latterly.org/dollar-shave-club-marketing-strategy/). _Note: the 50K/month figure is widely cited across marketing case studies but I could not source it back to a Dubin interview or filing — flag as "widely reported / case-study-tier source."_

### C. Acquisition
**Buyer:** Unilever. **Year:** July 19, 2016. **Price:** $1B cash. **Revenue at sale:** ~$153M trailing year, ~$200M projected for the year of acquisition. **Subscribers at sale:** 3.2M. **Multiple:** ~5x trailing revenue. Source: [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2016/07/19/unilever-buys-dollar-shave-club-for-1-billion/); [Retail Dive](https://www.retaildive.com/news/unilever-acquires-subscription-startup-dollar-shave-club-for-1b/423029/); [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2016/07/19/unilever-buys-dollar-shave-club-for-reported-1b-value/). **Why the buyer wanted them:** Defensive — Gillette (P&G) was hemorrhaging share; Unilever wanted the subscriber base, the disruptive marketing playbook, and DTC infrastructure it didn't have. Source: [HBR — Unilever's Big Strategic Bet on Dollar Shave Club](https://hbr.org/2016/07/unilevers-big-strategic-bet-on-the-dollar-shave-club). **Postscript:** Sold by Unilever to Nexus Capital Management Oct 2023 (Nexus 65%, Unilever retained 35%); price undisclosed, widely characterized as a write-down. Source: [Unilever press release Oct 2023](https://www.unilever.com/news/press-and-media/press-releases/2023/unilever-announces-the-sale-of-dollar-shave-club/).

### D. GESINE lesson
The launch video was DSC's permanent calling card — every subsequent funding round, retailer pitch, and press cycle referenced it for years. Spend disproportionately on one signature piece of launch content that can carry the brand for 24+ months, and make sure the founder is on camera.

## Cross-brand pattern for GESINE

Three of these five brands (Method, Hero, Touchland) were acquired by mass-CPG strategics (SC Johnson, Church & Dwight x2) in the $630M–$880M range at ~5–7x revenue. The shared pattern:

- A category they were unambiguously #1 or #2 in
- Founder still operating the business, willing to stay post-deal
- 30%+ EBITDA margin or a credible path to it
- A retail door count that the acquirer couldn't easily replicate from scratch
- A signature object/manifesto the consumer could describe in one sentence

GESINE's job in Phase 2 is to articulate, on one page, which of those five conditions it can plausibly satisfy in 36 months — and which acquirer's portfolio gap it is being built to fill.

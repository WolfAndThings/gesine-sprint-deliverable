## 1. Market Size — Verified

The deck's $258B (2022) → $312B (2025) figure is **accurate and from Coresight Research x GiftNow** (June 2022 study, "Unboxed: The $258 Billion US Corporate Gifting Opportunity"). 6.5% CAGR over 3 years. Re-circulated by Synchrony, PRNewswire, PPAI.

**Coresight breakdown (2022 base):**
- Business-to-Employee (B2E): 43% share, ~$110.9B
- Business-to-Business (B2B / client + partner): 57% share
- Gift cards alone: 36% of total (~$92.8B in 2022)

**Cross-check (other firms, broader definitions):**
- Business Research Company / GlobeNewswire: global market ~$886.6B (2025), projected $1.31T by 2030 at 8.2% CAGR. Includes APAC + retail-adjacent.
- ASI Central: US promotional products industry hit a record $26.6B in 2024 sales (a subset of corporate gifting, hard goods only).
- Snackmagic 2022 study cited a $242B global figure — narrower than Business Research Company.

**Use the Coresight $312B (2025) US-only number for the deck.** It is the most-cited figure in the trade press and is conservative versus global estimates.

## 4. Hospitality Amenity Buyers

**Marriott (which owns Edition):** Vendors apply via SupplierOne portal (marriott.supplierone.co). Marriott maintains approved-vendor lists for FF&E and amenities; PIP-compliant brands often required. EDITION is in Marriott's STARS ultra-luxury tier — wellness amenity decisions go through brand-level procurement, not individual properties.

**1 Hotels (SH Hotels & Resorts):** No public vendor-onboarding portal found. Sustainability-first positioning (FSC bamboo, refillable everything) aligns with GESINE. Path is direct outreach to corporate procurement / sustainability lead.

**Soho House:** Going private via MCR Hotels $2.7B deal (2025/26). Procurement consolidating. No public vendor portal.

**Aman:** Aman Essentials retail line is a public proof point that the brand sells curated home goods. No public vendor onboarding doc — direct relationship with Aman Essentials buyer.

**Standard / Ace:** No public source on amenity vendor process.

**Realistic path:** Hospitality is relationship-led, not portal-led. Source1 Purchasing aggregates hotel amenity procurement and is the fastest indirect entry. Direct sales should target Marriott EDITION + 1 Hotels first (sustainability fit).

## 5. Real Estate Closing Gifts

**Compass + Coldwell Banker + Sotheby's:** Compass is acquiring Anywhere Real Estate (parent of Coldwell + Sotheby's) in a $1.6B all-stock deal (CRE Daily, 2026). Combined entity = 340,000 agents.

**Buyer process:** Closing gifts are agent-funded, not brokerage-funded. Each agent picks vendors individually; brokerages do not run procurement. Average gift in trade press: $50-$200 typical, with luxury agents (Beverly Hills, Manhattan) running $500-$5K+. Coldwell Banker Warburg's published "33 Best & Worst" list shows agent preferences skew to: monogrammed cutting boards, branded tool boxes, wine, and "useful daily" items.

**GESINE fit:** Strong. The $78 Organizer Set hits the typical mid-tier gift band, and "first thing they unbox in the new home" is a defensible narrative. GTM is **agent-direct** via Compass/Sotheby's agent marketing portals + targeted ads to top-1% agents, not brokerage HQ.

## 9. Pricing Benchmarks — Where $78 Fits

Per GiftAFeeling, Postal, Giftsenda, Sparkgift 2024-25 data:

- Entry / mass employee: $15-$50
- Mid-tier client + employee appreciation: $50-$125 (77% of one-to-one gifts fall here)
- Top-tier client: $100-$500
- Executive / VIP: $500-$5,000+
- Recipient expectation gap: recipients expect ~$100; companies actually spend median ~$30

**GESINE $78 Organizer Set sits in the high-confidence mid-tier band.** Above the median actual spend, below the recipient expectation = perceived overdelivery. Strong psychological positioning.

## What This Means for GESINE

The two most analogous exits are Touchland (Church & Dwight, 2025, up to $880M / ~5.4-6.8x sales on $130M) and Hero Cosmetics (Church & Dwight, 2022, $630M / ~5.5x sales on $115M). Both are female-founded, design-led, scent or skin-adjacent, scaled DTC-then-retail, and EBITDA-positive at exit. Both went to Church & Dwight. That is the cleanest comp for GESINE's pitch: a luxury home reset bottle with EWG cred and a Crate & Barrel 2027 rollout slots into the same archetype, the same buyer, the same ~5x sales lane. Drunk Elephant's 8.5x sales (Shiseido, 2019) is the upside slide for "what happens if scent-led + clean lock the category". Aesop's $2.525B at L'Oréal (~4.7x on $537M) is the long-horizon ceiling. On the still-private side, Caraway, Blueland, and Homecourt are the live trajectory comps: Caraway because it shares the design firm and the non-toxic-home story, Blueland for the sustainability story at a $220M+ private mark, and Homecourt for scent-led luxury home with cult-brand growth. Investor target list should put Church & Dwight at the top (two recent home/personal-care deals in three years), Unilever second (six relevant deals 2016-2019, currently re-acquiring in wellness), SC Johnson third (the eco-home consolidator), and L'Oréal/Shiseido as premium-multiple upside acquirers if GESINE crosses ~$100M in net sales. On the venture side, Cult Capital (Homecourt), Sandbridge (DedCool), and Prelude Growth Partners (Blueland) are the warmest-fit growth investors for the Series A after the $400K angel round.

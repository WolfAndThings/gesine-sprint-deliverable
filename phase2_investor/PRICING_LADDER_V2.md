## GESINE proposed ladder

Three tiers. Every price `[unverified — recommend willingness-to-pay test before locking]`.

| Tier | SKU | Price hypothesis | Anchor reasoning |
|------|-----|------------------|------------------|
| **Trial** | Single Vial (10x18mm) | $18-22 | Floor sits below Branch Basics single concentrate ($19) but above Blueland refill ($2.25). Premium fragrance signaling. |
| **Trial** | 3-Vial Sample Box | $48-55 | Sub-$60 gateway. Justified vs Branch Basics $69 starter. |
| **Hero** | Hero Bottle (188x36x72mm, frosted glass) | $48-58 | Floor at Aesop Resurrection Hand Wash ($47). Premium for design-object density. |

| **Premium** | Discovery Set (2 bottles, 6 vials, organizer) | $195-225 | Sub-$250 gift threshold. Holiday + housewarming SKU. |
| **Premium** | Holiday Gift Box | $325-350 | Anchor near Aesop $300+ gift sets. Capture upper-end gifting. |
| **Top** | Founder's Edition (limited drop, 500 units) | $450-500 | Premium drop strategy. Press-bait. Aesop Resurrection Aromatique Hand Balm gift set hits this range. `[unverified — drop strategy assumption]` |

## Why this ladder works

1. **Trial under $25** lets a Gen Z buyer test the brand without committing. Same logic as Glossier's $14 Balm Dotcom built the funnel.
2. **Hero at $48-58** sits inside the "I'd pay this for hand wash if it looked great" Aesop floor.
3. **C&B Organizer at $78** is locked. Pulls retail-buyer trust + national distribution.
4. **Subscription annual at ~$250** compounds LTV. Closest comp: Branch Basics. See [REFILL_PROGRAM.md](REFILL_PROGRAM.md) for LTV math.
5. **Premium gift at $325** captures holiday + housewarming. Aesop owns this slot in beauty. White space in cleaning.
6. **Top tier $450-500** as drop. Limited press-driven, not core revenue. Drives "they make a $500 cleaning bottle" press story.

## What we don't do (and why)

| Move | Why we don't |
|------|--------------|
| Mass tier under $20 | Method and Mrs. Meyer's own this. Diluted brand. [research/brand_teardowns.md](research/brand_teardowns.md) shows Method capped at premium ceiling because of mass-priced floor. |
| Free shipping under $35 | Train customer to AOV at $50+. |
| Discount codes more than 15% | Erodes premium signal. Discounting beyond 15% = caps brand at "occasional sale" tier. |
| Heavy holiday discounting | Premium brands ship "exclusive holiday SKU" not "holiday discount on regular SKU." Aesop, Diptyque, Le Labo all follow this. |
| Subscription longer than quarterly default | Quarterly is the cadence Modern Premium Consumer expects (Apple iCloud, Goop GP, Branch Basics). Anything monthly feels like detergent, anything yearly loses repeat buy signal. |

The one-line thesis

GESINE is fragrance, not cleaner.
Fragrance is beauty.
The beauty consumer has no premium, EWG-conscious option for the home. We are it.

Home cleaning is the last category the beauty consumer hasn't been served. She buys Aesop hand soap at Nordstrom, a Canopy humidifier at Sephora, a Jolie filtered showerhead at Ulta, Touchland sanitizer at Ulta, Le Labo detergent online. Then walks past Method on the way home. GESINE collapses that gap with a fragrance-led, EWG-conscious, designed-to-be-seen cleaning ritual, sold where she already shops.

If they only remember three things

Beauty retailers are already buying our category. We are not asking them to invent a section. We are filling the gap inside it.

1. Sephora and Ulta already stock non-traditional beauty (Canopy at Sephora, Jolie + Touchland at Ulta, Saje, DedCool). Precedent set, no education needed.

2. EWG-verified products: ~2,500 in beauty vs ~150 in cleaning (EWG database, May 2026). Same consumer. Still a 15:1+ supply gap. We close it.

3. Touchland exited to Church & Dwight in Apr 2025 at $700M cash + up to $180M contingent earn-out. A sub-$15 personal-care SKU sold via Sephora. The exit math for our category is already on the board.

Section 01

The six pillars of the beauty case

Each pillar is a defensible answer to the investor's most likely pushback. None require the investor to take a leap of faith on category creation.

01

Fragrance is beauty.

US prestige fragrance hit ~$8B in 2024, growing ~12% YoY — fastest-growing beauty subcategory (Circana). 56% of fragrance buyers are under 35 — Gen Z and millennials are driving the surge. GESINE is fragrance-forward with seasonal scents. The product is a fragrance product that happens to clean — the way a candle is fragrance that happens to give light.

02

Beauty retailers already sell home / wellness.

Sephora wellness category up ~30% YoY since 2022 (analyst notes). Ulta launched dedicated wellness aisle 2022, expanded 2024. Sephora carries Canopy (humidifier), Touchland, DedCool. Ulta carries Jolie ($165 showerhead), Saje, Vacation. Bluemercury and Nordstrom carry Aesop home. The shelf already exists, we slot in.

03

EWG gap is the white space.

~2,500 EWG-verified beauty SKUs vs ~150 cleaning (EWG database, May 2026). The clean-conscious beauty buyer is trained to scan EWG and finds nothing premium in cleaning. GESINE is the obvious answer she's been priced into Branch Basics for.

04

"Skinification of home" is a named trend.

Premium home care growing ~8-9% CAGR vs ~2-3% for legacy (Mintel, Grand View). Global wellness home market: ~$200B (Global Wellness Institute). Beauty consumers apply skincare logic to home: ingredient panels, fragrance notes, ritual language. Jolie, Canopy, Branch Basics, Public Goods all ride it. We are early but not first — category is forming.

05

Beauty economics beat home-care economics.

Premium beauty gross margin runs 65-75%. Cleaning commodity runs 35-45%. Aesop hand soap retails $41.50; the same SKU at grocery would be $5. GESINE captures beauty margin on cleaning's repeat-purchase frequency — better LTV than serum.

06

The self-investment tailwind nobody is pricing in.

The GESINE buyer is spending more on herself, her ritual, and her home than any prior cohort. Cleaning has been re-coded as self-care. Self-care is sold in beauty aisles. Wellness is now the largest discretionary category in the US — and the consumer rebuilding her routine around it has nowhere premium to spend on the home. This is not a vibe shift — it is a balance-sheet shift.

US wellness market ~$1.8T

McKinsey 2024. Larger than US auto industry. Self-care = mainstream spend.

Premium home care CAGR ~8-9%

vs ~2-3% for legacy home care (Mintel, Grand View). Consumer is trading up.

Self-care spend per HH ~$200/mo

US average, up from ~$135/mo pre-2020 (NielsenIQ, McKinsey). Routine spend, not splurge.

Gen Z beauty spend ~$9B

US, +18% YoY (Piper Sandler "Taking Stock With Teens"). They will inherit the GESINE shelf.

Cleaning = self-care 41%

Of Americans say cleaning their home is a form of self-care (Cleveland Clinic / APA).

#CleanTok views 100B+

TikTok cumulative. Cleaning is now content, identity, and ritual — not a chore.

Millennial home spend ~$4K/yr

Per household, with ~40% on premium tier (Statista). Premium home is the default, not the splurge.

Bath & Body Works ~$7B

Annual revenue, public co (BBWI). Proves home fragrance + gifting at mass scale.

Section 03 · The white space

EWG-verified products: 15 to 1+ supply gap

The beauty buyer has been trained for a decade to scan ingredient labels and look for EWG certification. She finds plenty of options in skincare. She finds almost nothing in home care. Counts confirmed directly against the EWG Skin Deep + Healthy Living databases (May 2026).

Section 05 · In the room

Three questions to flip the meeting on offense

Do not spend the meeting convincing them cleaning can be beauty. Anchor on exit comps and distribution. These three questions reframe the conversation.

01

"Who in your portfolio sells in Sephora wellness — can you intro?"

Investor mode flips from evaluator to enabler. Beauty-focused funds have these relationships. Use them as a deal sweetener, not a hurdle.

02

"Touchland exited to Church & Dwight at $700M cash + up to $180M earn-out — what's your read on category-crossover M&A?"

Forces them to confirm the comp is real. Anchors expectations on the upside, not on whether the category will work. If they engage, they're already valuing us against that comp.

03

"Jolie went DTC → Ulta wellness in roughly 18 months. Realistic target for us?"

Lets the investor co-author the timeline rather than push back on it. Co-authoring is the closest thing to a soft commitment in a first meeting.

Section 06 · Guardrails

What not to say tomorrow

Three slips that historically cost cleaning brands their beauty-channel positioning. Easy to avoid.

Do not say

The phrases that frame us as a Method competitor instead of a beauty brand

  • "We compete with Clorox / Method / Mrs Meyer's." Anchors the buyer on $5 grocery price points and crushes our premium thesis. We compete with Aesop, Le Labo, DedCool, Diptyque.
  • "Non-toxic" / "chemical-free" / "100% safe." FTC and EWG guidance flag these. Use "EWG-conscious," "biodegradable," "thoughtfully formulated," "made without [X]".
  • "It's a cleaning product." It is a fragrance ritual that resets a space. The product description on the shelf and on the cap line should never lead with "cleaner."
The close

The beauty consumer already lives the GESINE thesis. We are giving her the SKU her shelf is missing.

She buys Aesop for the sink, Le Labo for laundry, Jolie for the shower, Touchland for her bag. The home-cleaning slot on her shelf is empty because no brand has shown up with the fragrance, the ingredient story, the design language, and the retailer-grade packaging at the same time. That is the GESINE shape.

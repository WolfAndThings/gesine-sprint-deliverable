◆ Who buys this, and the moment they reach for it
Four buyers. Eight rituals.
One bottle on the counter.
GESINE is bought by people who already own Aesop hand wash, a Diptyque candle, and a Caraway pan set. They are not searching for a cleaner — they are upgrading the last visible utility on the counter. This map names who they are, the moment that triggers a purchase, and the eight scent rituals each persona enters through.
◆ Persona 01 / 04
Design-Wellness Millennial
Aesthetic-Driven
28–38, urban, design-literate, treats home as identity
A+
LTV class · subscriber core
Demographic snapshot
$110K–$220K HHI. Brooklyn, Silver Lake, Mission, Wicker Park, Shoreditch. Reads Apartamento, Cereal, Kinfolk. Follows @aesopskincare, @flamingoestate, @byredo.
What they currently buy
Aesop hand wash, Diptyque candles, Le Labo Santal 33, Caraway cookware, Branch Basics or Blueland (begrudgingly — for utility only).
Trigger moments
New apartment. Hosting before guests arrive. Sunday morning reset. An Instagram post showing a beautiful kitchen counter. Restocking after a Branch Basics bottle finally empties.
Core objections
"Is this just a candle in cleaner-form?" "$80 is a lot for a spray." "Will the refill scent fade?" Solved by ingredient transparency + sample-size discovery set.
Pillar entry · LTV class
Enters through: D2C web · Apartamento collab · Aesop-adjacent IG content. LTV: $340/yr — bottle purchase + 4–6 refills + holder + gift-the-friend behavior. Most likely to subscribe.
◆ Winning hook
"The cleaning product designed to live next to your hand wash."
◆ Persona 02 / 04
Sunday Reset Buyer
Ritual-Driven
25–42, follows clean-girl & "that girl" aesthetic, ritualizes the week
A
LTV class · refill loyal
Demographic snapshot
$70K–$140K HHI. TikTok-native. Likely WFH or hybrid. Watches "morning routine" and "Sunday reset" content. Buys based on emotional regulation, not chemistry.
What they currently buy
Method, Mrs. Meyers, Grove Co., Touchland, Sol de Janeiro, Glossier. Hot tools. One nice candle. Recently upgraded their hand soap.
Trigger moments
Sunday morning. The night before a big week. The moment after a hard cry. Post-breakup. Cleaning a freshly-changed bedsheet. The end of the workday before partner gets home.
Core objections
"I already buy nice cleaners." "Is this just for show?" Solved by the scent-as-mood proposition + ritual content (Sunday reset reels). Price softened by $24 refill cadence.
Pillar entry · LTV class
Enters through: TikTok/Reels organic + UGC creator seeding. LTV: $260/yr — bottle + 5+ refills, occasional gift purchase. Highest organic share rate.
◆ Winning hook
"Cleaning, but it feels like a candle."
◆ Persona 03 / 04
Premium Gift Buyer
B2C Gifting
35–58, gives gifts > 12×/year, terrified of giving something tacky
A++
LTV class · AOV $140+
Demographic snapshot
$150K+ HHI. Hostess + housewarming + holiday + bridesmaid + thank-you-the-realtor + new-baby buyer. Already a Diptyque, Jo Malone, Flamingo Estate gift-set customer.
What they currently buy
Diptyque trio sets, Aesop gift boxes, Le Labo discovery sets, Goldune, Wax + Wick, Williams Sonoma, Pottery Barn gift cards (last resort).
Trigger moments
Housewarming party. Real estate closing — the agent gives a gift. Wedding gift roster. Holiday corporate exchange. Friend's first home. Mother-in-law's new apartment.
Core objections
"Is this weird to give as a gift?" Solved by gift packaging at retail + pre-built bundles ("New Home Reset", "Hostess Ritual") + a card slot in the box.
Pillar entry · LTV class
Enters through: Retail (Pottery Barn, Goop, MoMA Store) + Goldbelly + concierge gifting. LTV: $480/yr — 3–5 gift bundles annually. Highest AOV; lowest unsubscribe.
◆ Winning hook
"The housewarming gift that doesn't end up in a drawer."
◆ Persona 04 / 04
Clean Home Buyer
Ingredient-Driven
30–48, parent or pet-parent, EWG & SDS literate
B+
LTV class · converts on trust
Demographic snapshot
$90K–$180K HHI. Reads ingredient labels first, design second. Subscribes to Branch Basics, Blueland, Force of Nature. Has a Yuka or EWG app on their phone.
What they currently buy
Branch Basics concentrate, Blueland tablets, Puracy, Seventh Gen, Mrs. Meyers (if rushed). Not buying for design, but increasingly noticing it.
Trigger moments
New baby. New pet. Allergic reaction or a friend's news story. Kid crawling on counters. Restocking after a "non-toxic" cleaner ran out and the brand felt cheap.
Core objections
"Show me the ingredient list." "Is this an MLM?" "Greenwashing?" Solved by full transparency: EWG verification, fragrance disclosure, biodegradability sub, plastic-free claim.
Pillar entry · LTV class
Enters through: SEO ("non-toxic cleaner", "EWG cleaning") + parenting newsletters + Yuka/Think Dirty app integrations. LTV: $220/yr — bottle + steady refills, slow to upgrade but rarely churns. The trust persona.
◆ Winning hook
"The non-toxic cleaner you'll actually want to leave on the counter."
◆ The eight rituals

Eight scent rituals, eight buying moments.

Each ritual maps to a specific room, time of day, and emotional state. The persona match column is the persona most likely to enter through that ritual; the others stack as add-ons. This is the launch SKU map.

Nightstand
RITUAL 01
Persona match
Sunday Reset Buyer · Design-Wellness Millennial
Scenario beat
Right before bed. One spray on the nightstand and pillow vicinity. The cue that the day is over.
Product config
Scent: Quiet Cedar (woody, calming). Bottle: matte ceramic. Cadence: nightly · refill q.6–8 weeks.

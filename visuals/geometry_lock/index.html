HOW THE BOTTLE STOPS MUTATING
MAY 2026
The pipeline behind Round 2

The bottle is not generated.
The bottle is locked, then composited.

A clear, three-phase production pipeline. Exploration is generative. Final assets are not. Hero shots ship with the same sprayer height, same vial proportions, same cap radius across every scene. The mutation problem Ben flagged was real and it is structurally solved at Phase 2.

The problem · Ben's note

"The AI keeps mutating the product."

In Round 1, prompt-only generation drifted the sprayer height, vial neck, and cap radius across every shot. That is acceptable in mood exploration. It is unacceptable in a hero asset. A hero shot has to read as the same physical SKU across the entire retail and PR roll. Round 2 partially closed the gap. The Geometry Lock Workflow below is the structural fix that takes it from "mostly consistent" to "shot from a single bottle."

Phase 1 · exploration · what Round 1 + Round 2 are

Mood and art direction

Generative. The bottle is allowed to breathe.

The job of this phase is to find the lighting, palette, composition, and ritual language that lands the brand. Geometry drift is acceptable here because nothing in this phase is a final asset. Mood boards become reference packs. Reference packs feed the locked pipeline downstream.

2
Phase 2 · geometry lock · the structural fix

Product is locked, scene is composited

The 10 selected scenes are rebuilt with a fixed product.

The single highest-leverage move in the pipeline. The 10 selected hero compositions get the bottle, vial, and sprayer pulled out and replaced with the locked product asset. Same CAD-derived geometry in every shot. Same materials. Same proportions. The scene around the bottle stays. The product becomes a constant.

Source of truth
CAD file from Caraway design partnership. The actual physical bottle dimensions, sprayer geometry, vial proportions.
Render path
KeyShot studio render. PBR materials. Brushed hardware. Plain glass. Multi-angle library against the lighting from Phase 1.
Composite
Locked product dropped into the Phase 1 scene. Photoshop or Figma masking. Color grade matched to scene LUT.
Fallback
If the Phase 1 scene can't accept a clean composite, the scene gets a Stable Diffusion img2img pass with the locked render as ControlNet depth input. Geometry preserved.
Gate to Phase 3 All 10 hero scenes carry the same locked bottle, vial, and sprayer. Caliper test: trace the bottle silhouette from any two heroes and the lines should overlap. If they don't, the shot fails the gate.
3
Phase 3 · final asset production

Upscale, polish, and ship

The hero set hits print and retail spec.

The 10 locked scenes get the final pass. 4x upscale, color consistency sweep across the set, packaging integration where the gifting and retail scenes require it. Hero stills become source material for motion: the launch reel, the Crate & Barrel asset pack, the corporate gifting decks, the Sephora and Ulta pitch boards.

Upscale
Topaz Gigapixel or Magnific at 4x. Retail print spec is 300 dpi at 16 x 24 inches minimum.
Color sweep
Single LUT across all 10. Hero deck reads as one shoot, not ten generations.
Packaging
Gifting + retail scenes get the box, sleeve, and label artwork composited. Pulled from final dieline.
Motion
Hero stills feed Runway Act-One or Pika for the 6 to 12 second launch reel cuts. Same locked product, in motion.
Output 10 hero stills, retail-ready. 1 launch reel, motion-ready. 1 packaging pass on the gifting + retail subset. Path A and Path B share the hero library.

Round 1 + Round 2 · what we learned

Two rounds of exploration. Locked signal.

Strong directions and weak directions, called honestly. The strong list is the brief for Phase 2. The weak list is the reason the Geometry Lock Workflow exists.

Hold these · they are the brand

Strong directions

  • Luxury hard-flashEditorial flash with controlled spill. Reads as beauty editorial, not lifestyle stock.
  • Editorial beauty lightingSoft north-window key plus rim. The Aesop catalog cue. The frame she already trusts.
  • Clinical minimalismWhite or stone surfaces, single object, generous negative space. Reads premium without trying.
  • Warm neutral palettesCream, sage, oat, soft taupe. Anchored by one true black or one deep amber.
  • Ritual self-care framingHands in frame, half-finished gesture, ritual object adjacent. The frame Touchland and Aesop both run.
  • Hand-held intimacy shotsBottle in hand at 70 degrees, close crop. The shot that translates to TikTok and Reels without rework.
Stop generating these · Phase 2 fixes them

Weak directions

  • Product geometry driftSprayer height changes from frame to frame. Bottle shoulder varies by 10 to 15 percent. Fixed at Phase 2 lock.
  • Inconsistent sprayer proportionsSprayer head sometimes oversized, sometimes truncated. Reads as a different SKU on the same shelf. Fixed at Phase 2 lock.
  • Packaging distortionBox logo placement shifts. Label height drifts. Fixed when packaging composites pull from the locked dieline in Phase 3.
  • Overly surreal outputsMaximalist florals, over-dressed shelves, art-directed-into-the-ground compositions. Phase 1 selection criterion: would Aesop or Le Labo ship this. If no, kill it.
  • Bronze foil label cuesEarlier rounds added a bronze foil label that does not exist on the real bottle. Plain bottle and plain cap from Phase 2 forward. Bronze is hardware-only.
  • Stock-photo posingCentered, symmetrical, posed compositions. Reads stock, kills the prestige read. Phase 1 filter: editorial in-between or kill the frame.
The promise behind the pipeline

Round 3 is not another round of generation. Round 3 is final polish on a locked product.

The 10 heroes Lee and Ben pick from Round 2 do not get regenerated. They get the bottle, vial, and sprayer rebuilt against the CAD reference, dropped back into the same lighting and composition, color-graded as a set, upscaled, and shipped. From Round 3 onward, the product is a constant. Only the scene moves.

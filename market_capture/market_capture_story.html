01
Method
2001 → SC Johnson 2017
Adam Lowry + Eric Ryan · two San Francisco roommates.
Wealthy
angel
$90K + $1M
How they got their foot in the door$90K personal savings + friends-and-family to launch · then closed ~$1M from private investors led by ex-Yahoo chairman Tim Koogle. The Koogle check arrived when they had "$16 in the bank and $300,000 on their credit cards." Connected angel = Method's whole launch runway.
↓ The 5 actual moves
01
Hired Karim Rashid as Chief Creative Officer in 2001. The bottom-dispensing, bowling-pin-shaped clear bottle Rashid designed won an I.D. Magazine Annual Design Review Distinction and became the brand's pitch deck. The bottle WAS the marketing. SRC · Campaign · Champions of Design · CNN/Money 2004
02
First retail account = Mollie Stone's Market (suburban San Francisco, 2001). Two shelves of all-purpose spray. Tiny but essential proof-of-concept that funded the next pitch. SRC · Encyclopedia.com
03
Target 90-store trial in 2002 → 1,000+ Target stores in 2003. By 2003, Method was in ~10,000 doors doing ~$10M annual sales. This is the move that took Method from indie-grocery curiosity to national brand. SRC · Campaign · SBN Online
04
Co-founded the B Corp movement (May 2007 certification). Method was an original B Corp signatory — turned sustainability claims into a third-party-verified moat against legacy CPG. By 2017, Method + Ecover hit a B Impact score of 146 (top 2%). SRC · PR Newswire 2017
05
"People Against Dirty" voice + activist marketing. Method positioned the entire category as the enemy ("dirty business"), recruited values-driven customers instead of competing on price. Parent company is literally named "People Against Dirty." SRC · Fortune · Mopping Up With Method
↓ Acquisition
Buyer
Ecover (2012) → SC Johnson (2017)
Price
Both undisclosed
Revenue at sale
~$112M (Method standalone, 2012); ~$200M combined w/ Ecover at SC Johnson sale
Why they bought
SC Johnson needed sustainability-credentialed brand portfolio to defend against shifting consumer preferences in home care.
GESINE lesson
Hire one senior design name early and let one signature object carry the brand through every door. Caraway-firm bottle = GESINE's Karim Rashid bottle. The product silhouette is the pitch deck.

01
Kickstarter, 2018: 1,304 backers · $67,622 raised against $15K goal (450% of goal · 150% in first 24 hours). Manufactured demand and generated the proof Sephora later required. SRC · Kickstarter · Create & Cultivate · Lisbona interview

5-year compounding revenue: ~$12M (2020) → 163% growth Q1 2023 → 203% growth FY2023 → $100M+ (2024) → $130M TTM at acquisition. Not one viral moment — a five-year drumbeat. SRC · GCI Magazine · Cosmetics Design

Hero Cosmetics
2017 → Church & Dwight 2022
Ju Rhyu (CEO) + Dwight Lee + Andrew Lee · founded 2017. Rhyu discovered the K-beauty hydrocolloid pimple patch as an expat in Seoul working at Samsung.
Co-founder
pool
$50K
How they got their foot in the door$50,000 pooled by the three co-founders. No outside money. No friends-and-family round. Three people put in their own savings, sold one product on Amazon, and grew the brand to $115M revenue in 5 years before C&D bought it for $630M. The leanest origin in the comp set.
↓ The 5 actual moves
01
Amazon Prime-eligibility pricing hack. Rhyu wanted to price Mighty Patch at $9.99 but couldn't unlock Prime. Raised price to $12.99, qualified for Prime, and the first batch sold out in 90 days. Prime alone is reported to lift orders ~25%. SRC · Fortune · Amazon Prime "accident"
02
Dominate Amazon's "acne patch" search SERP — go all-in on one channel before diversifying. Hero's Amazon dominance built the trust signal and review volume that later made retailers come to them. SRC · Female Startup Club podcast
03
TikTok #SkinTok virality powered by the "peel-off reveal" mechanic. Mighty Patch was inherently a content product — creators peel the patch and show the absorbed gunk underneath. Rhyu credits social as the key driver of brand popularity. SRC · Fortune · Gen Z status symbol · Glossy 50 · 2022
04
Target launch September 2019: 1,521 doors with a SKU built specifically for that retailer ($7 Mighty Patch Duo: 6 Original + 6 Invisible+). Designed an exclusive retail-channel pack rather than dumping the DTC SKU into mass. Revenue grew 300%+ in 2020 alone after the Target launch. SRC · Beauty Independent · PR Newswire
05
Stay narrow until you own the category, then expand. Hero stayed purely on hydrocolloid acne patches until #1 acne-patch + #2 acne-brand position in US — only then expanded into adjacent SKUs (cleansing bars, dark spot patches). Over 1 billion patches sold. SRC · Fortune · BeautyMatter
$50K → $630M
5 years · 3 founders · 1 SKUHero pooled $50K from three co-founders in 2017. Sold to Church & Dwight for $630M in 2022 — at 14x EBITDA. The narrowest playbook in the comp set.

Drunk Elephant
2012 → Shiseido 2019
Tiffany Masterson · Houston-based mother of four · no prior beauty industry background · concept 2012, brand officially launched August 2013.
Bootstrap
→ PE Series A
$0 → $8.33M
How they got their foot in the doorPure bootstrap from 2012 to 2017. No friends-and-family round disclosed. Masterson sold her first products herself, was discovered at Cosmoprof 2014, launched in Sephora January 2015. First outside capital didn't arrive until March 2017 — a minority investment from VMG Partners + Leandra Medine of Man Repeller (~$8.33M). Two years later sold to Shiseido for $845M.
↓ The 5 actual moves
01
The "Suspicious 6" manifesto: essential oils · drying alcohols · silicones · chemical sunscreens · fragrances/dyes · SLS. A bannable, repeatable, trademarked list became the brand's spine. It defined enemies, made the product instantly explainable, and let customers self-identify as "Suspicious-6-free" loyalists. SRC · FOLIO.YVR · Drunk Elephant homepage
02
2014 Cosmoprof trade show → Sephora discovery → January 2015 Sephora launch. Masterson didn't pitch Sephora — she went to a trade show, was spotted, and was invited in. Became one of Sephora's top-selling and fastest-growing skincare brands almost immediately. SRC · Fashionista
03
"Biocompatible" positioning that rejected the binary "natural vs synthetic" frame. Drunk Elephant publicly argued "natural" doesn't automatically mean good — every ingredient must directly benefit skin health. Gave Sephora consumers a third option between Whole Foods naturals and prestige synthetics. SRC · Drunk Elephant Philosophy
04
The "smoothie" routine concept and "Trunk" merchandising bundle. Reframed skincare as a mix-and-match cocktail rather than a fixed routine; Trunk gave Sephora a high-AOV gifting/holiday SKU. SRC · Space NK · Trunk
05
Hero product anchor: Virgin Marula Luxury Facial Oil. A single signature SKU carried brand identity (Marula → "Drunk Elephant" name itself) and gave press, influencers, gifters one thing to talk about. Won Influenster's 2018 Reviewers' Choice + 2019 Best Indie Facial Oil. SRC · Sephora · Influenster
↓ Acquisition
Buyer
Shiseido (via Shiseido Americas)
Date
Announced Oct 8, 2019 · closed Nov 6, 2019
Price
$845M
Revenue / multiple
~$100M (Shiseido cited 2019 net sales ~$120M) · ~8.5x revenue · among the highest multiples paid for a premium-DTC beauty brand

GESINE lesson
Name your enemies. The "Suspicious 6" gave Drunk Elephant a manifesto a customer could repeat at a dinner party. GESINE needs an equivalent banned-list — a short, memorable enumeration of what luxury cleaning has been doing wrong (synthetic fragrance · plastic refills · "cleaning theater" marketing) that a customer can recite back.

Dollar Shave Club
2011 → Unilever 2016
Michael Dubin (CEO, ex-improv comedian, ex-Sports Illustrated digital) + Mark Levine (met at a holiday party where Levine mentioned a warehouse of cheap razors). Concept Jan 2011, service launched March 6, 2012.
Tier-1 VC
seed
$1M seed + $9.8M A
How they got their foot in the doorDubin used his agency network + the viral video to land tier-1 institutional money fast. $1M seed in March 2012 from Kleiner Perkins + Andreessen Horowitz + Shasta Ventures + Forerunner Ventures (one of the earliest DTC seed deals). $9.8M Series A in October 2012 — same syndicate plus Venrock. No friends-and-family. No bootstrap. Straight to top-tier VC because the $4,500 launch video had already done the convincing.
↓ The 5 actual moves
01
The $4,500 viral launch video, March 6, 2012. Written by and starring Dubin, shot in one day. Within 48 hours: 12,000 new subscribers. YouTube traffic crashed the company server in the first hour. Won "Best Out-of-Nowhere Video Campaign" 2012 Ad Age Viral Video Awards. Now past 27M views. SRC · Inc · "How a $4,500 YouTube Video Turned Into a $1B Company"
$4,500
One video · 4 years · $1B exitDubin spent $4,500 on the launch video. 12,000 subscribers signed up in 48 hours. Server crashed in the first hour. Every later funding round, retailer pitch, and press cycle pointed back to that one video.
02
Subscription model with a $1/month entry tier. "$1" was the headline hook; unit economics worked off the $6 and $9 tiers. Subscriber count compounded to 3.2M by acquisition. SRC · Retail Dive · Fortune
03
"Bathroom Minutes" — a printed mini-magazine in every shipment (2014-2015). ~10-page editorial newsletter combining articles, product news, community signals. Turned a delivery box into a media moment and meaningfully reduced churn. SRC · Contently
04
MEL Magazine launched late 2015 — full editorial site for men's content. Long-form pieces, short documentaries, real masthead. DSC became a media company that happened to sell razors. SRC · Convince & Convert
05
Refer-a-friend program: $5 credit per successful referral, prompted right after first purchase. Drove ~50,000 referrals/month at peak. SRC · Latterly · case study (widely cited; no Dubin interview / filing source surfaced — flag as case-study tier)
↓ Acquisition
Buyer
Unilever
Date
July 19, 2016
Price
$1B cash
Revenue / multiple
~$153M trailing year · ~$200M projected for year of acquisition · 3.2M subscribers · ~5x trailing revenue
Why they bought
Defensive — Gillette (P&G) hemorrhaging share. Unilever wanted the subscriber base + the disruptive marketing playbook + DTC infrastructure it didn't have.
Postscript
Sold by Unilever to Nexus Capital Oct 2023 (Nexus 65% / Unilever retained 35%) · price undisclosed · widely characterized as a write-down.
GESINE lesson
The launch video was DSC's permanent calling card — every subsequent funding round, retailer pitch, and press cycle referenced it for years. Spend disproportionately on one signature piece of launch content that can carry the brand for 24+ months — and make sure the founder is on camera.
The pattern across all 5

Three of these (Method · Hero · Touchland) sold to mass-CPG strategics for $630M-$880M at ~5-7x revenue.

SC Johnson · Church & Dwight × 2. The shared shape of each acquisition tells GESINE exactly what conditions need to be true 36 months from now.

01
A category they were unambiguously #1 or #2 in. Hero = #1 acne patch · Touchland = #2 hand sanitizer · Method = #1 design-led eco home.
02
Founder still operating the business · willing to stay post-deal. Andrea Lisbona at Touchland · Ju Rhyu at Hero · Eric Ryan + Adam Lowry at Method.
03
30%+ EBITDA margin or a credible path to it. Touchland 42% · Hero 40% · the multiple won't show up below this floor.
04
A retail door count the acquirer couldn't easily replicate from scratch. Method 1,000+ Target stores · Hero 1,521 Target doors · Touchland 2,500+ doors at acquisition.
05
A signature object or manifesto the consumer can describe in one sentence. Method's Karim Rashid bottle · Drunk Elephant's "Suspicious 6" · Touchland's TikTok pastel mist · DSC's $4,500 video · Hero's peel-the-patch reveal.
